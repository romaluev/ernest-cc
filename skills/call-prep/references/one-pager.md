# Call one-pager — structure

Render this to the digest. Keep each section to a few lines; the CEO scans it in
the 2 minutes before the call. Order is fixed.

```markdown
# Call prep — <company> · <date/time>

## Bottom line
<Call goal in one line + the single thing that makes it a win.>

## Who's on the call
- <Name> — <role>; cares about <motivation>. (source)

## Deal context
- Stage / amount / owner: <…> (HubSpot)
- History & past agreements: <…> (calls, mail)
- Open items / promises owed: <…>

## Goal & exit criteria
- Goal: <…>
- Success looks like: <concrete next commitment>

## Discovery questions
1. <4–6 sharp questions that advance the deal>

## Positioning (Higgsfield fit)
- <2–3 points tied to their pains — video/creative/enterprise where it fits>

## Competitive context
- <Who else is in play; how we differentiate>

## Risks & objections
- <Objection> → <our answer>

## Next steps
- <What to lock by end of call; who owns follow-up>

## Sources
- CRM: <…> · Calls: <…> · Web: <…> (mark what is inferred)
```

Notes:
- If account is graded (Tier-1/2/Trash), put the tier next to the title.
- "deep" depth adds a short market/competitor scan; "quick" omits web research.
